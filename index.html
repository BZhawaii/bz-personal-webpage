About

I am a software developer who was born with a love for problem-solving. As a boy living in Hawaii, I loved puzzles, riddles, and brain teasers. As I grew older, I became a paperboy and I applied my problem-solving skills to modify the new customer compensation package to sign up many first time customers, which got me second place in the paperboy of the year competition

In my twenties and thirties I worked at UPS, where I learned the value of hard work and working in a team. I also used my problem solving skills to satisfy the many different needs of my customers based on our single product. When I moved to the mainland, I became the owner of a trucking and delivery company as a contractor for FedEx. Being a business owner, I really got to flex my problem-solving muscles. One of my biggest 'wins' was in addressing my need for seasonal drivers. I hired three firefighters on differing schedules and combined them into one full time driver position. It was a technique that was duplicated by other contractors the following year.

Four years ago, my wife was hired into a faculty position at CSU in Fort Collins, CO. Upon moving there, I decided on a career change. I got my BS in IT from CSU, graduated from Galvanize's 6-month full stack immersive program, and finished a 6-week Java/Spring class.

Now I'm taking all my old skills and combining them with all my new skills to create business solutions that are based around programming solutions. I love creating new and innovative solutions that help solve problems and make people's lives better.

So far I've taken those skills and embarked on a few new adventures. During the Fort Collins Startup Startup Weekend at CSU, I was part of the team that won first place, by creating a viable business solution to the problem presented to us. Later in the year, I was a speaker at the Loveland Startup Weekend and presented a talk called 'Web Development for the Absolute Beginner'. I'm currently working on a side project, with a CSU Doctoral Candidate, to try and create assistive technology to assist people with social-emotional agnosia.
